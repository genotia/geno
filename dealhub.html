Is this deal still valid?

A deal's score is never one person's opinion. Every listing passes through four checkpoints: how it's sourced, how the crowd verifies it, how confidence is labeled, and how contributors earn the right to be trusted, before you'd ever see it as reliable.

01. Nothing arrives anonymously
●
Source is always disclosed
Every deal states where it was found: Instagram, WhatsApp, a photo of a poster, DealDrop, word of mouth, and more. No deal shows up without a paper trail.
●
A fair start, not a sprint
New deals sit in a short grace period before their score goes public, so a rival business, or an overexcited friend, can't snipe or inflate it in the first minutes.
●
Attribution shapes starting trust
Community find, personal tip, or verified Merchant post via DealDrop: each carries a different starting confidence level, never treated the same.
02. The crowd verifies it, weighted by trust
●
Votes are weighted, not just counted
A Hunter, Expert, or Legend-tier member's vote counts for up to 8× a brand-new account's, so the score reflects earned trust, not just traffic.
●
Evidence beats a click
Every vote can carry a verification comment, so "still valid" or "expired" comes with a real explanation attached, not just a silent tally.
03. Redemption is the highest signal
●
"I redeemed" outweighs everything else
Someone who actually walked in and used the deal counts for double a normal vote, because they're the only one with real proof.
●
Gold badge at scale
Once 5 or more people confirm they've redeemed a deal, it earns a gold "Redeemed" badge: the clearest signal DealHub can give.
04. Confidence is labeled, never hidden
●
Every deal is tagged plainly
Verified, High confidence, Disputed, or still in its Grace period: trust level is visible before you ever click into a deal.
●
Deals are re-checked, not just posted once
A live expiry countdown and an ongoing "is this still valid?" prompt catch deals that quietly go stale after launch.
●
Contributors are publicly accountable
A fully transparent points ledger (including penalties for deals flagged as fake or found expired within the hour) plus a public leaderboard, so reputation is earned and visible, not claimed.
